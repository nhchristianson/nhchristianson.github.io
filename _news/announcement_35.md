I had a great time attending ACM e-Energy in Banff this week! We had two full papers accepted to the main conference: one on [online and learned algorithms for thermal energy network control](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3744255.3811734), and one on [risk-sensitive and learning-augmented algorithms for peak-aware energy scheduling](https://dl.acm.org/doi/abs/10.1145/3744255.3811739) (<strong class="caltech-orange">best paper finalist!</strong>). In addition, I co-organized the workshop on [Physics-Informed Learning for Optimization and Control of Susatainable Energy Systems](https://learning-for-energy.github.io/), and gave an award talk for the SIGEnergy dissertation award. It was great to see so many collaborators and friends!
